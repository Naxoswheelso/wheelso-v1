We are committed to processing your data in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the "GDPR") and Greek Law 4624/2019.

2. What personal data we collect

2.1 Data you give us

  • Identification: first and last name, date of birth, national ID or passport number, nationality.
  • Driving credentials: driving licence number, issuing country, issue and expiry dates, licence categories, years held.
  • Contact: email address, mobile phone, country code; postal address if you request an invoice.
  • Travel information: flight or ferry number, arrival time, pick-up / return station and time, additional driver details.
  • Billing: business name, VAT number and tax office where you request a corporate invoice.
  • Communications: the content of any email, chat or call we exchange, and any notes you ask us to record.

2.2 Data from your payment

Payments are processed by our payment service provider. We do not store full card numbers; we receive only a payment token, the last four digits, the card brand and expiry month/year, the amount, currency and authorisation result.

2.3 Data from the vehicle

  • Vehicle condition reports: photographs and notes taken at pick-up and return.
  • Accident / damage data: police reports, third-party details, witness statements, repair invoices.
  • Anti-theft GPS: where a vehicle is fitted with a GPS unit for fleet anti-theft purposes, location data is collected. It is consulted only on a suspicion of theft, breach of the rental terms, or in connection with an accident.

2.4 Data we collect automatically

  • Website usage: pages visited, referring URL, time on page, search parameters, booking funnel events.
  • Technical: IP address (truncated where possible), browser type and version, operating system, device type, time zone, approximate location derived from IP.
  • Cookies: see our Cookies Policy for the full list.

3. Why we use your data & on what legal basis

We rely on the following legal bases under Article 6(1) GDPR:

3.1 To perform the rental contract — Art. 6(1)(b)

  • Process bookings, confirmations, modifications and cancellations.
  • Verify identity and licence eligibility at pick-up.
  • Hand over the vehicle, record its condition, and handle return.
  • Provide roadside assistance and customer service.
  • Process payments and issue receipts/invoices.

3.2 To comply with legal obligations — Art. 6(1)(c)

  • Tax and accounting record-keeping (Hellenic Tax Code, Law 4308/2014).
  • Compulsory motor insurance (Law 489/1976).
  • Forwarding of traffic fine notifications to the responsible driver.
  • Responding to lawful requests from courts, police, or supervisory authorities.

3.3 Legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)

  • Preventing fraud, theft and unauthorised vehicle use.
  • Managing claims and recovering amounts owed for damage, fines or unpaid rentals.
  • Securing our website and IT systems (logs, abuse detection).
  • Improving our services through aggregate analytics.
  • Sending service-related communications about your existing booking.

You may object to processing based on our legitimate interests at any time on grounds relating to your particular situation; see Section 6.

3.4 Your consent — Art. 6(1)(a)

  • Optional marketing emails about offers and seasonal promotions.
  • Non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing) — managed via our cookie banner.

You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

4. Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with carefully selected recipients, on a need-to-know basis:

4.1 Service providers (processors)

  • Hosting & infrastructure: Netlify Inc. (USA) for static website hosting, and Railway Corp. (USA, EU region) for application servers and managed PostgreSQL database. Servers are located in the EU where possible; some control-plane traffic may be processed in the United States.
  • Payments: Viva Wallet S.A. (Greece) — PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant payment service provider authorised by the Bank of Greece. Card data is collected directly by Viva via a hosted payment page and is not stored on our servers. We receive only a payment confirmation and a masked card token for reconciliation.
  • Transactional email: Resend Inc. (booking confirmations, receipts, account communications).
  • Cookie consent management: CookieYes Ltd (United Kingdom) — stores your cookie preferences and consent proof in line with the GDPR and the ePrivacy Directive.
  • Roadside assistance: Extra Assistance (for the limited purpose of carrying out a roadside intervention).
  • Repair partners: authorised garages, in case of damage.
  • Insurers and brokers: for compulsory insurance and damage claims.
  • Accountants and auditors: bound by professional confidentiality.
  • Legal advisors and debt-recovery agents: only as needed for specific cases.

4.2 Public authorities

We may disclose data to the police, courts, tax authorities, customs, the Hellenic Data Protection Authority and other authorities where required by law, court order or a lawful request, in particular in connection with traffic violations, accidents, theft or tax inspections.

4.3 International transfers

Some of our processors (for example for hosting or email delivery) may transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area, in particular to the United States. Such transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards under Articles 44–49 GDPR — typically the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914) and, where applicable, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. You may request a copy of the safeguards in place by contacting us.

5. How long we keep data & how we protect it

5.1 Retention periods

  • Booking and rental contract data: 5 years from the end of the calendar year of the rental, to meet our tax-law obligations (Law 4308/2014); extended where required by ongoing claims or litigation.
  • Tax records, invoices, accounting documents: at least 5 years, in line with the Hellenic Tax Code; up to 20 years where related to ongoing audits or disputes.
  • Damage / accident / fine files: for the duration of the claim plus the applicable limitation period (typically up to 5 years; up to 20 years for bodily-injury claims).
  • Marketing consent and related data: until you withdraw consent or after 24 months of inactivity, whichever comes first.
  • Website logs: up to 12 months.
  • CCTV at stations (where installed): 15 days, then automatically overwritten.

5.2 Security

We apply technical and organisational measures appropriate to the risk, including: encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest where applicable; role-based access control to the booking and admin systems; strong authentication for staff accounts; logging of administrative actions; regular backups and patching; written confidentiality undertakings with employees and processors. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we strive to apply the best practices appropriate to the size and nature of our operations.

6. Your data-protection rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights, which you may exercise free of charge:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — receive a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — request deletion, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18) — limit processing in specific situations.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) — receive data you provided in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct-marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.
  • Right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing. Wheelso does not currently take any decisions about you on a purely automated basis without human involvement.

6.1 How to exercise your rights

Write to hello@wheelso.gr or to our postal address (Section 1) with your request. We may ask you to verify your identity before responding. We will reply within one month, extendable by two further months for complex requests under Art. 12(3) GDPR.

6.2 Right to lodge a complaint

If you believe our processing of your personal data infringes the GDPR, you may lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority of your habitual residence, place of work or place of the alleged infringement. The competent authority in Greece is the Hellenic Data Protection Authority (ΑΠΔΠΧ): 1–3 Kifissias Avenue, 115 23 Athens, telephone +30 210 6475600, website dpa.gr, complaint form at dpa.gr/el/polites/katagelia_stin_arxi. We would welcome the opportunity to address your concerns directly before you contact the Authority.

7. Cookies, children & updates

7.1 Cookies and similar technologies

Detailed information on the cookies we use, their purposes and how to manage them is set out in our Cookies Policy. You can change or withdraw your cookie consent at any time by clicking the cookie preferences icon at the bottom-left of any page, or by visiting our Cookies Policy.

7.2 Children

Our services are directed to adults aged 21 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can erase it.

7.3 Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, services, or applicable law. The "Last updated" date at the top will indicate the version in force. Where changes are material, we will give you reasonable advance notice by email or via a notice on the website.

  • Δικαίωμα διαγραφής (άρθρ. 17) — να ζητήσετε διαγραφή, υπό την επιφύλαξη νόμιμων υποχρεώσεων διατήρησης.

  • Δικαίωμα φορητότητας (άρθρ. 20) — να λάβετε δεδομένα που μας δώσατε σε δομημένη, ευρέως χρησιμοποιούμενη μορφή.
  • Δικαίωμα εναντίωσης (άρθρ. 21) — να αντιταχθείτε σε επεξεργασία που στηρίζεται σε έννομα συμφέροντα ή έχει σκοπό απευθείας marketing.
