Pickup Locations in Paros

Paros National Airport (PAS)

We meet you at the arrivals area with your keys ready. The airport is located 9 km south of Parikia and serves daily flights from Athens plus seasonal European routes. Average pickup time is 5 minutes.

Parikia Port

If you arrive by ferry from Piraeus, Mykonos, Santorini or Naxos, we meet you at the port — a 3-minute walk from the ferry dock. Parikia is the busiest port in the Cyclades after Mykonos, with daily connections to most islands.

Hotel, Villa & Naoussa Delivery

Free delivery to any hotel or villa in Parikia, Naoussa, Pounta, Aliki, Drios, Lefkes, and other main areas. For Antiparos delivery, please contact us in advance — we can coordinate the ferry crossing for you.

Driving in Paros: Local Tips

Roads & Beach Access

Main roads connecting Parikia, Naoussa, and the airport are fully paved and easy to drive. Access roads to popular beaches like Golden Beach, Kolymbithres, and Pounta are paved. Some remote southern beaches (Faragas area) have unpaved sections — a small SUV is recommended for these.

Naoussa Parking

Parking inside Naoussa old town is extremely limited. Use the public parking at the village entrance and walk down — it takes 3 minutes and saves you a lot of stress, especially in July and August.

August Traffic

From mid-July to late August, expect heavier traffic on the Parikia–Naoussa road and around major beaches between 11:00 and 13:00 (people heading to the beach) and 17:00–19:00 (returning). Driving outside these hours is much faster.

Distances from Parikia

Naoussa: 11 km · Pounta: 7 km · Golden Beach (Chrysi Akti): 17 km · Lefkes: 9 km · Aliki: 10 km · Drios: 18 km · Paros Airport: 9 km · Ferry to Antiparos (from Pounta): 7 min.

Night Driving

Many rural roads outside main villages have limited street lighting. Drive carefully at night, especially on the mountain road to Lefkes and the southern coast roads. Watch for goats and stray cats — they appear suddenly.

Fuel Stations

Most fuel stations are concentrated in Parikia and along the Parikia–Naoussa road. If you plan a long day in the south or a trip to Antiparos, fill up in advance.

Going Windsurfing or Kitesurfing?

Paros is one of the top windsurfing destinations in the Aegean, with Pounta and Golden Beach (Chrysi Akti) hosting world-class conditions in July and August. If you are bringing or renting surf gear, ask us about cars with roof racks or larger boots. We can also recommend the best timing to drive around the Pounta channel when winds peak.

Rental Requirements in Paros

  • Minimum age: 21 years for standard cars, 25 years for SUV and 4x4 vehicles.
  • Driving licence: valid licence held for at least 1 year. EU and international licences accepted. Non-Latin licences (Cyrillic, Chinese, Arabic, etc.) require an International Driving Permit.
  • ID: passport or national ID.
  • Payment: credit or debit card in the main driver's name (Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay, Google Pay).
  • Deposit: a security deposit is held on your card during the rental. The amount depends on the car category.
  • Antiparos crossing: please inform us in advance if you plan to take the ferry to Antiparos.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I pick up my rental car in Paros?

You can pick up at Paros National Airport (PAS), Parikia Port, or get free delivery to your hotel or villa anywhere on the island.

Do I need a car for beach hopping in Paros?

Yes — a car is essential. Most guests drive daily between beaches like Golden Beach, Kolymbithres, and Pounta. Buses exist but are limited.

Is parking difficult in Naoussa?

Yes. Use the public parking at the village entrance and walk in. Trying to drive inside the old town is not recommended, especially in summer.

Can I take the ferry to Antiparos with my rental car?

Yes. The small ferry from Pounta to Antiparos runs every 30 minutes and accepts cars (7-minute crossing). Please notify us in advance.

What is the minimum age to rent in Paros?

21 years for standard cars, 25 years for SUV and 4x4. Licence held for at least 1 year.

Is fuel included in the price?

No. We operate a full-to-full policy: you receive the car with a full tank and return it full.

What happens in case of a breakdown?

All Wheelso rentals include 24/7 roadside assistance. Call the number on your rental agreement and we will reach you anywhere on Paros (or Antiparos).
